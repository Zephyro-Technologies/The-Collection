Brand guidelines
01 — How we speak

Quietly, and with certainty.

The Collection speaks the way it sells: without noise, and without haste. We state what is true about a car — its provenance, its condition, what has been verified — and then we stop. The strongest claim is the one that does not strain to be heard. Privacy is the product. We never shout, never discount, never oversell.

02 — Black, white & silver

Neutral blacks, warm whites, cool silver.

The blacks are strictly neutral (r=g=b). The whites are warm. The accent is a cool brushed silver. That split is the character of the identity — do not tidy it into a single temperature. There is no gold in this brand.

04 — The accent is a gradient

Brushed metal, used sparingly.

On the website the silver arrives as a gradient, not a flat colour. It fills the primary call to action and is clipped into large display numerals. Never set body copy in it — the gradient's darkest stop is only AA-large. The dashboard uses flat steel and no gradient.

05 — Signals

Semantic colour is not the accent.

Status colour is chromatic and stays separate from the silver. Each surface needs its own value — a signal tuned for a white card fails on a black one.

06 — Wordmark usage

One lockup, two inks.

The script "The" over a condensed Didone "COLLECTION". It is monochrome: white artwork on dark surfaces, black artwork on light ones. The lockup is 1.69:1 — set a height and let the width follow. Give it clear space of at least the cap-height of "COLLECTION" on every side.

Do

White lockup on noir or graphite. Black lockup on alabaster or white.

Never

Never recolour it, never reintroduce a gold "COLLECTION", never stretch it — the aspect is fixed at 1.69:1.

Outstanding from the client: a vector (SVG) and a square mark. A 1.69:1 wordmark is not legible at favicon size; the current favicon is an interim crop of the Didone "C" from the lockup.

07 — The car is the hero

The brand in use.

Photography carries the colour; the interface never competes with it. Surfaces are black, type is white, and the only shine in the room is the silver on a price or a call to action. If a layout feels decorated, remove something.
